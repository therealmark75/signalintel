# The Ledger

Three weeks is enough time to memorise a routine and to begin, quietly, to hate it. Mara knew now which boards on the quay would creak, which clerk drank his lunch, and which window in the company office was never latched.

She also knew the word in the destination column. She had found it printed, once, on the underside of a crate lid: not a place after all, but a name. A person's name. And the weights in the ledger were not cargo.

## What the Weights Meant

She did the arithmetic three times because she did not want it to be true. A barrel of salted fish weighs what a barrel of salted fish weighs. These did not.

When she finally understood, she sat very still, the way you sit when a wasp lands on the back of your hand and your whole body becomes a single held breath. Then she stood, smoothed her coat over the hidden pages, and walked out into a town that suddenly seemed to be watching her back.

The *Kestrel* was due again on Thursday. She had until Thursday to decide what kind of person she was going to be.
